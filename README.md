# Edge Computing Architectures

## Introduction

This project documents an AI-only workflow for creating a technical article and
its follow-on presentation assets.

The team has three members. Each member used a different AI tool to produce an
initial article on the shared topic, "Edge Computing Architectures." One of
those article drafts was created with Codex support.

The project treats prompt engineering as the only tool of engagement with the
AI systems involved, and prompts are tracked deliberately and chronologically
as a core part of the project record.

The working rules for the article phase were strict:

- No manual editing of document content.
- No copy-paste of content between documents.
- No manual correction of wording or structure.
- The minimum valid output unit is a human-readable and AI-readable document.

Using those rules, the team produced three initial source documents. Those
documents were then combined with Claude into the current master document:
[master_article.pdf](/home/raven/data/doc/privat/uni/predmeti/4-ita/promptathlon/artifacts/master_article.pdf).

## Strategy

The project strategy is to use prompt engineering as the only intervention
mechanism and to keep every substantive output as a document artifact rather
than a manually edited intermediate state.

Three team members each used a different AI system to generate an independent
article on the same topic with similar prompts. This created parallel starting
points that could be compared for overlap, differences in framing, and possible
weaknesses.

The comparison step is used to identify:

- recurring ideas that appear across multiple outputs,
- distinct perspectives that add useful coverage,
- mistakes, inconsistencies, or unsupported claims.

The strategy also includes explicit verification because AI-generated material
can contain incorrect facts or fabricated references. Relevant sources are
checked against academic and technical sources before they are relied on.

After comparison and verification, the independent outputs are synthesized into
the master article.

## The Goal

The current goal of the project is to create an approximately 10-minute video
presentation that explains how the master article was created from scratch.

The presentation should focus on the process, not only on the topic itself. It
should show how three independent AI-generated article drafts were produced,
how they were combined into the final master article, and how prompt
engineering guided the work throughout the process.

The team intends to use free tools only. Based on the current assumption that
free AI video-generation tools often limit output to roughly 1 to 3 minutes per
video, the final presentation may need to be produced as three separate video
segments to reach the target duration.
